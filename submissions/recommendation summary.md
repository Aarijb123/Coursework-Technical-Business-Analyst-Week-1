## Recommendations

Phase 1 should focus on OP-03 Eligible payment-plan selection, OP-02 Digital promise-to-pay capture, and OP-05 Rules-based routing as the primary opportunities.

These rank highest because they directly address the most significant breakdowns in the current recovery process. OP-03 and OP-02 are the main value drivers, as they increase the likelihood that customers move into sustainable repayment arrangements and ensure those commitments are properly captured and followed through. OP-05 is critical alongside this because it determines whether cases are handled in the right way from the start, reducing wasted effort caused by cases which are incorrectly routed to agents. Together, these three opportunities have the strongest combined impact on both recovery performance and operational consistency.

The lower-ranked opportunities such as OP-01 Self-serve account summary, OP-04 Contact detail update request, and OP-06 Case validation—are being deferred because they are primarily enabling improvements rather than direct drivers of financial or operational step-change. While they clearly address real friction in the process, their impact is either dependent on customer adoption (OP-01) or only fully realized once the core decisions and routing issues are fixed (OP-04 and OP-06). Delivering them in Phase 1 would improve individual parts of the journey, but would not resolve the structural issues that currently limit recovery performance.

From a future-state workflow perspective, Phase 1 should establish a more structured end-to-end recovery journey built around consistent decisions and automation at key points. OP-05 defines how cases are routed into the correct pathway, OP-03 ensures customers are guided toward the most appropriate repayment option, and OP-02 ensures any agreement made is formally captured and tracked. This creates a more controlled and predictable operating model, reducing reliance on manual judgement for routine decisions.

The results of Phase 1 should directly shape the future-state design by shifting the process away from a fragmented, agent-led workflow towards a rules-driven system where agents focus primarily on exceptions and complex cases. Once this foundation is in place, later phases can focus on improving efficiency and experience layers (such as self-service, data quality, and validation controls) without risking the integrity of the core recovery flow.
